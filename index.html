Mario Francis Puzo

An author, screenwriter and journalist

Here is a timeline of Mario Puzo's life.

  • 1920-Mario Francis Puzo was born on October 15 in New York city, US.
  • He graduated from NY' city college and therafter joined the US Airforce and participated in World War Two.
  • In 1950, his first short story was published in American Vanguard, after which his first book(The Dark Arena) was published in 1953.
  • He is known for his crime novels about the italian-American Mafia and silican mafia notably The Godfather.
  • The novel(the Godfather) remained on newyork times best seller list for 67weeks and sold over 9 million copies in two years.
  • 1972-The book was later developed into a film, directed by Francis Fond Coppola. The film received 3 awards of the oscar category nominations, including Puzo's oscar for Best Adapted Screenplay.
  • 1973-He won the Golden Globe Award as Best Screenplay.
  • 1999-Puzo died of heart failure on July 2nd at his home in bayshore, newyork at the age of 78.
  • Although the godfather remains his best known work, other works of him are: The last don, Omerta, The fortunate Pilgrim, Silican, amongst others.
